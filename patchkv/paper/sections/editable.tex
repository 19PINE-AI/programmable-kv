\section{Consequence I: the cache is editable}
\label{sec:editable}

\paragraph{Naive editing fails---by design.} The mechanism predicts the in-place edit will fail, and it
does: refreshing the field's KV while reusing the stale downstream leaves the decision at the old value
(\Cref{fig:teaser}b). The prefix before the field is genuinely reusable (deviation $0.0$); the problem is
that the conclusion was already memoized after it.

\paragraph{The robust fix is an erratum, not recomputation.} If the stale notes carry an old conclusion,
the cheapest correct intervention is to append a \emph{salient erratum} that supplies the corrected state
late in the context---``\texttt{[STATE UPDATE] field $\rightarrow$ new; overrides any earlier value and
conclusion}''---so the decision token attends to a fresh, authoritative note. Appended after the original
field (\texttt{field+erratum}), this matches the strong \emph{hoist-to-end} baseline's oracle correctness
\emph{without rewriting the prompt}: under chain-of-thought the erratum recovers the flipped decision at
$0.97$ on Qwen3-8B (\Cref{fig:edit}a), and the edit is append-only, so it composes with prefix caching
(\Cref{sec:systems}).

\paragraph{When the ${\sim}1\%$-compute edit alone suffices: a reasoning/scale law.} Surprisingly, under
explicit reasoning the cheap in-place edit \emph{by itself} can recover the decision, because the
chain re-reads the (now-refreshed) field and re-derives the conclusion rather than trusting the stale
notes. This recovery is strongly model-size dependent (\Cref{fig:edit}b): field-only reasoning recovery
falls as the memoized conclusion becomes ``stickier'' with scale, so the minimal amount of selective
recomputation needed, \fieldsel{}@$K$ (refresh the field plus the $K$ highest-effect downstream tokens),
varies from $K^\star\approx 4$ at $8$B to $>64$ at $4$B (\Cref{fig:edit}c). Without reasoning, no small
$K$ helps ($0.00$ recovery at every scale). \fieldsel{}@$K$ is therefore a genuine but
\emph{unreliable} surgical tool---effective when the conclusion is not sticky, model-dependent otherwise;
we report it honestly rather than as a default.

\paragraph{A frontier, not a winner.} A controlled comparison (full table in the appendix) shows no single
dominant method. Hoist-to-end is cheapest but demands prompt surgery and pre-identification of every field;
\texttt{field+erratum} matches it with no surgery at a one-line append; \inplace{} is near-free but only
under reasoning; a KV-deviation-ranked selective recompute (CacheBlend-style) underperforms here because it
chases changed keys rather than the tokens that \emph{memoized the conclusion}. The practical
recommendation is \texttt{field+erratum} as the robust default, with \inplace{} as a free fast-path for
reasoning models.
